Learning how to protect yourself

Knowledge is your first defence

The image above is a photo of Delft,Cape Town. It is known as one of the most dangerous areas in Cape Town and is amongst the top 5. It is beaten by Nyanga, Khayelitsha and Phillipi. If you live in any of the following areas, below are some tips and tricks to protecting yourself.

Satying safe in your home

In these areas where public displays of money might put a target on you back so a security alarm is not an option, here are other ways to remain safe.

  • Get to know your neighbours and form a neighborhood watch group.

    • Forming a watch group can help protect your neighborhood by reporting suspicious activity and remain alert of strange happenings.

  • Secure doors and windows

    • Putting up locks and on doors and windows will deter break ins and if there are attempts, they will be loud and give you time to protect yourself.

    This also allows you to make safe spaces in your house so that you can protect you family and all your valuables.

  • Practising vigilance

    • Pay attention to all your entry ways to you home as well as teaching your children about making sure doors and windows are closed. Be mindful of ways you house can be entered without your knowledge or consent.

Safety while walking an commuting

Commuting from these areas can be very challenging and very dangerous at night.Any of use know this, as it has been estimated that 40% of persons above the age of 16 have taken many steps to protect themselves when commuting at night.

  • Stay vigilant

    • When walking, especially at night, make sure to pay attention to your surrounds whne traveling.Do not walk while on your phone or with earphones. You do not have to be on high alert at all times, however ensuring you're not distracted in order to scope out potential threats. Make to know where you are going all the alternative routes you can take avoid danger while trying to stick to well lit areas.

  • Share your route with others

    • Make sure to tell someone you test the possible routes you will be taking. In the worst case scenerio , if you were to go missing they would know when to look first. Furthermore, trust you instincts when walking, if you ever feel like you are in danger, find a public place for safety.

  • Valuables and non-lethals

    • When walking , make sure to secure your valuables in non-visible places, only keeping immediate needables such as keys and non-lethal detterents. Please make sure to get a non-lethel detterents, whether it be pepper spray, extendable batons or tasers. These things can be purchase under R150.

Crime Statistics in South Africa (2025)
  • Murder: While Q1 2025 recorded a 3.4% to 6.5% decrease, over 6,400 to 6,953 murders were still reported in some three-month periods.
  • Overall Crime Level: South Africa ranks second in the continent for criminality in 2025, with an organized crime risk score of 7.43/10.
  • Violent Crime (Q3 2025): 11,481 rapes, 7,710 attempted murders, and 49,418 cases of serious assault were reported in the last three months of 2025.
  • Commercial Crime: Increased by 4.7% in the fourth quarter, with the highest numbers in Gauteng (12,074) and the Western Cape (7,244).
  • Reduction Areas: Declines were noted in some areas, including carjacking (12.3% lower) and residential robberies (14.5% lower) compared to 2024.
  • Public Safety Perception: Only 36.1% of South Africans feel safe walking alone at night.
  • Households: An estimated 983,000 households were victims of housebreaking, with 43.3% of people taking measures to guard against crime, such as installing security, up from 39.9% in 2024.
